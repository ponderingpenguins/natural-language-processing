This study has a few limitations. First, we only evaluate on AG News, so our conclusions may not transfer to datasets with different writing styles or label structures. Second, our robustness analyses have some limitations: the length bucket analysis is based on word count rather than token count, which may not perfectly capture the model's handling of input length, especially for models with different tokenization strategies. Also, the keyword masking probe relies on TF-IDF to identify important keywords, which may not capture all the nuances of what the model considers important for classification. Additionally, for the LSTM we used mean pooling to fix converging issues with the final hidden state representation, but this may not be the optimal way to represent the sequence for classification, and other pooling strategies could yield different results. Finally, we only compare a single LSTM architecture and a single transformer architecture (DistilBERT), so our conclusions about the superiority of transformers may not hold for all possible architectures or configurations of these models.
